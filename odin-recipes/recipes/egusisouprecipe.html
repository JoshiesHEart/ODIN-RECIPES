Egusi Soup Recipe

Description

Egusi soup is a rich and tasty nigerian soup made from ground melon seeds, vegetables, fish, and assorted meats. It is loved for its thick texture, delicious flavor,and satisfying aroma, making it a popular dish in Nigerian cuisine. The soup is often enjoyed with fufu, pounded yam, or eba, which are traditional Nigerian starchy sides. Egusi soup is a staple in many Nigerian households and is often served during special occasions and family gatherings. It is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, making it a favorite among many.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup ground egusi seeds
  • 2 cups vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in the ground egusi seeds and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Gradually add the water while stirring to prevent clumping.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the egusi is cooked through.
  7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Serve with fufu, pounded yam, amala, or eba.
